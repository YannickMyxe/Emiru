Normal sentences

In Japanese a sentence does not contain any spaces, question marks, exclamation marks. Evry word just follows opon each other. There are some cases where these are used, they use it mostly when they are writing for non native Japanese speakers to make it easyer for them to read and seperate words.
A basic Japanese sentence is made as follows: subject - wa - sentence - desu(or another verb). Where Wa is just the toppic marker, while desu(pronounced as [des]) just means to be. Example:

Emiru: Watashi wa Emiru desu >> I am Emiru.

In Hiragana: わたし は えみる です
In katakana: ワタシ ハ エミル デス


A typical Japanese Question

A Japanese yes or no question is very simple, just add Ka at the end of your sentence.

Yanniku: Anata wa Emiru desu ka? >> Are you Emiru?

In Hiragana: あなた は えみる です か
In katakana: アナタ ハ エミル デス カ

To awnser this question you can say Yes or No. In Japanese this is simply hai and iie respectivly.
Back to our original question:

Yanniku: Anata wa Emiru desu ka?
Emiru: Hai

And that's it you just responed to your first question! Well done!
Now let's get a better awnser than just yes
So knowing how to make a basic sentce we would reply with: Yes, i am Emiru or No, i am not emiru
This time we will say Yes, I am Emiru.

Yanniku: Anata wa Emriu desu ka?
Emiru: Hai, Watashi wa Emiru desu.

Well done! But what if we asked you if you are someone else? How would you awnser now? Let's take the easy route first, just say No, I am Emiru.

Yanniku: Anata wa Sakurai desu ka? >> Are you Sakurai? In wich case you awnser:
Emiru: Iie, Watashi wa Emriu desu.

Well done! Now lets say you are not Sakurai instead, you just simply take a normal sentence and instead of desu you add dewa arimasen

Yanniku: Anata wa Sakurai desu ka?
Emiru: Iie, Watashi wa Sakurai dewa arimasen.

Good job! Now you can politly say that you are not someone.
