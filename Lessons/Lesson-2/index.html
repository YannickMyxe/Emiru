Normal sentences


In Japanese a sentence does not contain any spaces, question marks, exclamation marks. Evry word just follows opon each other. There are some cases where these are used, they use it mostly when they are writing for non native Japanese speakers to make it easyer for them to read and seperate words.
A basic Japanese sentence is made as follows: subject -

わ Wa
This is the topic marker of the sentence.
- sentence -
です(or another verb). desu [des]
To be
Example:
Emiru: Watashi wa Emiru desu Translation: I am Emiru.


In Hiragana: わたし は えみる です
In katakana: ワタシ ハ エミル デス


A typical Japanese Question


A Japanese yes or no question is very simple, just add Ka at the end of your sentence.
Now we only need

You, for now lets use Anata. More on personal pronouns and how to adress someone in a later lesson.


Yanniku: Anata wa Emiru desu ka? Translation: Are you Emiru?


In Hiragana: あなた は えみる です か
In katakana: アナタ ハ エミル デス カ

To awnser this question you can say
はい hai - Translation: Yes
or
いいえ iie - Translation: No
. Back to our original question:

Yanniku: あなた わ えみる です か
Anata wa Emriu desu ka?
Are you Emiru?
Emiru: はい Hai
Yes


And that's it you just responed to your first question! Well done!
Now let's get a better awnser than just yes
So knowing how to make a basic sentce we would reply with: Yes, i am Emiru or No, i am not emiru
This time we will say Yes, I am Emiru.

Yanniku: あなた わ えみる です か Anata wa Emriu desu ka?
Are you Emiru?

Emiru: はい, わたし わ えみる です Hai, Watashi wa Emiru desu.
Yes, I am Emriu.


Well done! But what if we asked you if you are someone else? How would you awnser now? Let's take the easy route first, just say No, I am Emiru.

Yanniku: あなた わ さくらい です か Anata wa Sakurai desu ka?
Are you Sakurai?

Emiru: いいえ, わたし わ えみる です Iie, watashi wa Emriu desu.
No, I am Emiru.


Well done! Now lets say you are not Sakurai instead, you just simply take a normal sentence and instead of desu you add
でわ ありません dewa arimasen
not to be


Yanniku: あなた わ さくらい です か Anata wa Sakurai desu ka?
Are you Sakurai?

Emiru: いいえ, わたし わ さくらい でわ ありません Iie, Watashi wa Sakurai dewa arimasen.
No, I am not Sakurai.



Good job! Now you can politly say that you are not someone.
Now lets say you are not Sakurai but you are Emiru.

Yanniku: あなた わ さくらい です か Anata wa Sakurai desu ka?
Are you Sakurai?

Emiru: いいえ, わたし わ さくらい でわ ありません. いわたし わ えみる です Iie, Watashi wa Sakurai dewa arimasen. Watashi wa Emiru desu.
No, I am not Sakurai. I am Emiru.
